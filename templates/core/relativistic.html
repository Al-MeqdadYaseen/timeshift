📚 Understanding Special Relativity

What is time dilation?

When an object moves at speeds close to the speed of light, time passes slower for that object relative to a stationary observer. This isn't just a theory, it's been measured experimentally with atomic clocks on airplanes and particles in accelerators.

The Formula

The time dilation factor (γ, or gamma) is calculated as:

γ = 1 / √(1 - v²/c²)

Where:

  • v = velocity of the moving object
  • c = speed of light (we measure v as a fraction of c)
  • γ (gamma) = how many times slower time runs

Examples from everyday life

  • Walking speed (0.0000001c): γ ≈ 1.000000000000005 — completely unnoticeable
  • Satellite speed (0.00003c): γ ≈ 1.0000000005 — GPS satellites must correct for this
  • Particle accelerators (0.999999c): γ ≈ 707 — particles live much longer than they "should"

The closer you get to light speed, the more dramatic the effect becomes. At 99.9999% of light speed, time runs 707 times slower!
