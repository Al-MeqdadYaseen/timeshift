📚 Understanding Gravitational Time Dilation

What is gravitational time dilation?

Einstein's theory of general relativity predicts that time passes slower in stronger gravitational fields. The closer you are to a massive object, the slower time moves relative to a distant observer. This isn't just theory, it's been measured and must be corrected for GPS satellites to work accurately.

The Formula

The time dilation factor depends on mass and distance:

factor = √(1 - 2GM/rc²)

Where:

  • G = gravitational constant
  • M = mass of the object
  • r = distance from center
  • c = speed of light

The result is a number between 0 and 1 that tells you what fraction of "normal" time passes.

Examples from the universe

Object Dilation Factor What it means
Earth's surface 0.9999999993 Time runs 0.00000007% slower — measurable but tiny
Sun's surface 0.9999989 Still small, but 1000x stronger than Earth
White dwarf 0.9997 Noticeable effect: time runs 0.03% slower
Neutron star 0.77 Time runs 23% slower — very noticeable
Black hole (near horizon) 0.1 Time runs 90% slower — extreme!

Near a black hole's event horizon, time slows to almost a standstill. If you watched someone fall in, they'd appear to freeze in time.
